# Google Forms
**Vendor:** Google
**Description:** Google Forms is a web-based application that allows users to create surveys, quizzes, and questionnaires. It enables users to collect and organize information efficiently, offering various question types and customization options for different needs. Responses can be reviewed and analyzed within the application or exported to other tools like Google Sheets.
**Product Website:** https://docs.google.com/forms

### AI Generated Insights
Google Forms generally provides accessibility features like screen reader support and keyboard shortcuts, making it usable for individuals with disabilities in many scenarios. However, various reports highlight challenges, particularly concerning image accessibility, color contrast, and the cognitive load associated with complex form creation. Third-party analyses and best practice guides consistently recommend thoughtful design choices by form creators to enhance accessibility, such as using clear titles, providing meaningful image descriptions, and ensuring adequate color contrast. While the product supports basic accessibility, the ultimate accessibility of a Google Form often depends on how well the creator implements inclusive design principles. Some functional limitations, like difficulties in accessing response summaries with screen readers or issues with link text, have also been noted.

### AI Generated Insights
Google Forms presents a mixed landscape regarding accessibility, with both inherent strengths and notable challenges that require careful consideration during form creation. While Google provides official VPATs and support documentation for screen reader users, indicating a commitment to accessibility, many third-party insights highlight persistent issues. Common concerns revolve around proper labeling of form fields, particularly in complex question types like Likert scales, and inconsistent focus order for keyboard navigation. Developers and institutional users often need to implement best practices and workarounds to enhance the accessibility of Google Forms, as direct code modification is not an option. The platform's ease of use for creating forms is a significant advantage, but creators bear a substantial responsibility in designing forms with accessibility in mind to ensure equitable access for all users.
